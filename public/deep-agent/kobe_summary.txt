Kobe Bryant (1978‑2020) was a five‑time NBA champion (2000‑2002, 2009, 2010) with the Los Angeles Lakers and a two‑time Finals MVP (2009, 2010). He earned the league’s regular‑season MVP in 2008, was a 18‑time All‑Star (1998‑2016) and won four All‑Star Game MVP awards (2002, 2007, 2009, 2011). Bryant captured two NBA scoring titles (2005‑06, 2006‑07) and famously scored 81 points in a single game against Toronto in 2006. Internationally, he won an Olympic gold medal with Team USA at the 2008 Beijing Games. Off the court, Kobe was a prolific storyteller and philanthropist: he authored several children’s books, founded the Mamba Sports Academy, and earned an Academy Award in 2018 for his animated short “Dear Basketball.” He was a champion for youth education, supporting numerous charities and serving as a mentor to players worldwide. His legacy endures through his “Mamba Mentality,” inspiring dedication, resilience, and excellence across sports and beyond.
