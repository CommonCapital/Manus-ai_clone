# Kobe Bryant – Career Accomplishments

## Overview
Kobe Bean Bryant (1978‑2020) was an American professional basketball player who spent his entire 20‑year NBA career with the Los Angeles Los Angeles Lakers (1996‑2016). He is widely regarded as one of the greatest players in basketball history.

| **2000** | Indiana Pacers | **Kobe Bryant** (co‑MVP with Shaquille O'Neal) | First championship; Kobe’s breakout postseason performance (15.6 ppg, 5.4 reb, 2.2 ast). |

**Total NBA Championships:** **5** (2000‑2002, 2009, 2010)

## Career Milestones & Records
- **Points:** 33,643 (4th all‑time at retirement; now 5th). 
- **Games Played:** 1,346 (tied for most games with a single franchise). 
- **All‑Time Lakers Scoring Leader** (tied with Kareem Abdul‑Jabbar). 
- **Most Points in a Single Game:** 81 points vs. Toronto Raptors (Jan 22 2006). 
- **Only player in NBA history to score 60+ points in a game on five separate occasions.** 
- **First player to start his career with a 5‑year contract (1996) and then sign a $136 million extension (2007).** 
- **Two‑time Olympic gold‑medalist.** 
- **Inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame** (Class of 2020, announced in 2016). 
- **NBA 75th Anniversary Team** (2021).

---

## Off‑Court Accomplishments
- **Academy Award (2018)** – Oscar for Best Animated Short Film for "**Dear Basketball**" (the first Oscar won by a professional athlete).
- **Author** – Wrote several children’s books, including *The Wizenard Series* and *Mamba Mentality: How I Play*. 
- **Philanthropy** – Co‑founded the **Mamba & Mambacita Sports Foundation**; supported youth sports, education, and disaster relief.
- **Business** – Partnered with Kobe Inc. on ventures in sports apparel, media, and investment.

---

## Legacy
Kobe Bryant’s impact extends beyond his statistics. His "**Mamba Mentality**" – a relentless drive for excellence, work ethic, and resilience – has become a cultural touchstone. He inspired generations of athletes, creators, and entrepreneurs worldwide. His tragic death in a helicopter crash on Jan 26 2020 led to an outpouring of global tributes, underscoring his lasting influence on and off the court.
