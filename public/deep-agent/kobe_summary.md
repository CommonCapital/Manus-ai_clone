Kobe Bryant (1978‑2020) is celebrated as one of basketball’s most prolific talents. Over a 20‑year NBA career with the Los Angeles Lakers, he captured five championships (2000‑2002, 2009, 2010) and earned two Finals MVPs (2009, 2010). Although he never won a regular‑season MVP, Bryant was a 18‑time NBA All‑Star, securing four All‑Star Game MVP honors (2002, 2007, 2009, 2011). Internationally, he contributed to Team USA’s gold medals at the 2008 Beijing Olympics and the 2012 London Olympics, reinforcing his global impact.

Beyond the court, Kobe’s influence extended into business and philanthropy. He co‑founded the sports‑app venture Granity Studios, authored the Academy Award‑winning short film *Dear Basketball* (2018), and published the children’s book series *The Wizenard Series*. His “Mamba‑Mentality” ethos inspired countless athletes and entrepreneurs, emphasizing relentless work ethic and resilience. Kobe’s post‑retirement contributions earned him the 2020 Sports Emmy for *The Last Dance* documentary series and numerous charitable awards. His legacy persists through mentorship programs, youth sports initiatives, and a lasting imprint on basketball culture worldwide.
