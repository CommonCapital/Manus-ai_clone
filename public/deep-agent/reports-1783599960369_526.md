### Summary
- **Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary**  
  - Defines **“internet of things”** (abbrev. **IoT**) as *the connection of devices within everyday objects via the internet, enabling them to share data*.  
  - Provides pronunciation guides (`/ nt net v z/` and `/ nt rnet v z/`).  
  - Example usage: “From smart refrigerators to web‑enabled teddy bears, the internet of things has never been more extensive.”  
  - Indicates the term is listed under **A1** level word lists and appears in the “Word of the Day”.  

- **NIST Computer Security Resource Center (CSRC) Glossary**  
  - Defines **IoT devices** as *user or industrial devices that are connected to the internet, including sensors, controllers, and household appliances*.  
  - Highlights that IoT devices comprise hardware, software, firmware, and actuators that allow devices to connect, interact, and exchange data.  
  - Lists relevant NIST publications where the definition appears (e.g., NIST SP 800‑172, SP 800‑160 Vol. 2, SP 800‑53 Rev. 5, etc.).  
  - Provides a note on contacting source authors for terminology questions.  

*Both sources present the core definition that IoT is the network of everyday objects/devices connected to the internet for data sharing.*
