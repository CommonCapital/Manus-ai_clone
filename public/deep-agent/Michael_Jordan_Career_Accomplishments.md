# Michael Jordan – Career Accomplishments (Quick Reference)

## NBA Career
- **Teams:** Chicago Bulls (1984‑1993, 1995‑1998), Washington Wizards (2001‑2003)
- **NBA Championships:** 6 (1991‑1993, 1996‑1998)
- **NBA Finals MVP:** 6 (one per championship)
- **Regular‑Season MVP:** 5 (1988, 1991, 1992, 1996, 1998)
- **All‑NBA First Team:** 10 selections
- **All‑Defensive First Team:** 9 selections
- **NBA All‑Star:** 14 selections (1985‑1993, 1996‑1998, 2002, 2003)
- **Scoring Titles:** 10 (1987‑1993, 1996‑1998)
- **Career Averages:** 30.1 points, 6.2 rebounds, 5.3 assists per game (career)

## Major Statistical Records & Milestones
- **Highest career scoring average** in NBA history – 30.1 ppg.
- **10× scoring champion** (most in league history).
- **30‑point games:** 1,251 (most in NBA history).
- **Career playoff points:** 5,987 (most in NBA history).
- **Three‑peat champion** twice (first player to win three consecutive titles twice).
- **Game‑winning shot:** 1982 NCAA Championship, 1998 NBA Finals Game 6, etc.
- **41‑point game** in his final NBA appearance (2003).
- **First player to win NBA MVP, Finals MVP, and All‑Star Game MVP in the same season** (1995‑96).

## International Play
- **Olympic Gold Medals:** 2
  - 1984 Los Angeles – Team USA (college player)
  - 1992 Barcelona – "Dream Team" (NBA star)
- **FIBA World Championship:** Gold, 1992 (exhibition, part of Dream Team)

## Off‑Court Achievements
- **Philanthropy:**
  - $100 M pledge (2020) to combat racial inequality and support community initiatives.
  - $7 M donation to University of North Carolina for scholarship fund (2021).
  - Numerous charitable contributions via the Jordan Brand and personal foundations.
- **Business Ventures:**
  - **Jordan Brand (Nike):** Launched 1984; generated >$3 B annual revenue, making him the most successful athlete‑endorsed brand.
  - **Charlotte Hornets:** Principal owner (2010‑present), overseeing franchise growth.
  - **Stakeholder in Sports‑related investments** (e.g., sports nutrition, esports).
- **Hall of Fame:** Inducted into Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame (2009) and College Basketball Hall of Fame (2006).
- **Cultural Impact:** Recognized as global icon; featured on numerous documentaries, books, and the 1996 "Space Jam" film.
