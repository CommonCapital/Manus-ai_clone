LeBron James – Career Accomplishments (Summary)

- **NBA Championships (4)**: 
  • 2012, 2013 – Miami Heat (defeated Oklahoma City Thunder and San Antonio Spurs)
  • 2016 – Cleveland Cavaliers (historic comeback vs. Golden State Warriors, 3‑1 series deficit)
  • 2020 – Los Angeles Lakers (won in the NBA bubble, defeating Miami Heat)

- **NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) Awards (4)**:
  • 2008‑09, 2009‑10 (Cleveland), 2011‑12, 2012‑13 (Miami). LeBron is tied for second‑most regular‑season MVPs in league history.

- **All‑Star Selections (19)**:
  • Selected every year from 2005‑2024 (except 2021 due to COVID‑19 protocols), tying the record for most career All‑Star appearances.
  • 3× All‑Star Game MVP (2006, 2008, 2018).

- **Scoring Title (1)**:
  • 2007‑08 – Led the league with 30.0 points per game, becoming the first player since Michael Jordan to win a scoring title while also being an MVP candidate.

- **Notable Records & Milestones**:
  • All‑time leader in NBA playoff points (≈ 7,500) and most playoff minutes played.
  • Youngest player to reach 30,000 career points (33 years, 24 days).
  • First player in NBA history with 30,000 points, 10,000 rebounds, and 10,000 assists.
  • Only player with a triple‑double in an NBA Finals game (2015, 2nd Game vs. Golden State).
  • Holds the record for most consecutive seasons with at least 1,000 points, 500 rebounds and 500 assists (19 seasons).

- **Other Major Achievements**:
  • 2× Olympic gold medalist (2008, 2012) and 2021 bronze medalist.
  • 13× All‑NBA First Team, 5× All‑NBA Second Team.
  • Named to the NBA 75‑th Anniversary Team (2021).
  • Ranked #1 on ESPN’s 2020 list of the 100 Greatest NBA Players of All Time.

These highlights encapsulate LeBron James’ impact as one of the most versatile, durable, and record‑setting players in basketball history.
