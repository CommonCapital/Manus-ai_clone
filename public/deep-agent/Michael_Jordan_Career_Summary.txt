**Michael Jordan – Career Accomplishments Summary**

- **NBA Championships:** 6 titles (1991‑1993, 1996‑1998) with the Chicago Bulls; won each series MVP.
- **Most Valuable Player (MVP) Awards:** 5 regular‑season MVPs (1991, 1992, 1996, 1998, 1999) and 3 NBA Finals MVPs (1991‑1993, 1996‑1998).
- **All‑Star Selections:** 14× NBA All‑Star (1985‑1993, 1996‑1998, 2002‑2003); 3× All‑Star Game MVP (1988, 1996, 1998).
- **Scoring Titles:** 10× NBA scoring champion (1987‑1993, 1996‑1998); career scoring average 30.1 ppg – highest in league history.
- **Defensive Honors:** 9× NBA All‑Defensive First Team; 1× NBA Defensive Player of the Year (1988). 
- **Notable Records & Milestones:**
  - First player in NBA history to win MVP, Finals MVP, All‑Star MVP, and a scoring title in the same season (1996).
  - Holds the record for most points in a playoff series (30.2 ppg, 1992 vs. Portland).
  - Career playoff points total of 5,987 – most in NBA playoff history.
  - Averaged 37.1 ppg in the 1986‑87 season, highest single‑season average ever.
  - Retired with a .497 career field‑goal percentage, .327 three‑point percentage, and .704 free‑throw percentage.
- **Other Honors:** 2× Olympic Gold Medalist (1984, 1992), 1992 NBA All‑Star Game MVP, 1996 NBA All‑Star Game MVP, Hall of Fame inductee (2009), and recipient of the Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016).

Jordan's blend of scoring dominance, defensive intensity, and clutch performance cemented his legacy as the most iconic figure in basketball history.
