Michael Jordan, widely regarded as the greatest basketball player of all time, built an iconic career in the NBA primarily with the Chicago Bulls (and a brief stint with the Washington Wizards). Over 15 seasons he scored 32,292 points (30.12 ppg), earned 5 regular‑season MVP awards, and captured six NBA championships (1991‑1993, 1996‑1998), each accompanied by a Finals MVP. Jordan was a 14‑time All‑Star, won ten scoring titles, and was named to the All‑Defensive First Team nine times. Internationally, he won two Olympic gold medals—first in 1984 as a college star and again in 1992 as a member of the legendary Dream Team. Off the court, Jordan revolutionized athlete endorsements, launching the Air Jordan sneaker line and becoming a global brand ambassador that expanded the NBA’s worldwide appeal. He founded the Charlotte Hornets franchise (now owner), contributed to charitable causes through the Michael Jordan Foundation, and was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame as a player in 2009 and as part of the 1992 Dream Team in 2010. His competitive drive, leadership, and cultural impact have set enduring standards for athletes and transformed basketball’s place in popular culture.
