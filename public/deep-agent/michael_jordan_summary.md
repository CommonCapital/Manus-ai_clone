Michael Jordan, widely regarded as the greatest basketball player of all time, captured six NBA championships with the Chicago Bulls (1991‑1993, 1996‑1998) and earned Finals MVP honors in each of those victories. He was a five‑time regular‑season MVP (1991, 1992, 1996, 1998, 1999) and a 14‑time NBA All‑Star (1990‑1993, 1996‑1998, 2002‑2003). Jordan claimed ten scoring titles, leading the league in points per game in 1987‑1993, 1996‑1998, and 1999‑2000, and he also won the 1988 Defensive Player of the Year award. His career milestones include retiring with a .327 career field‑goal percentage, a then‑record 30,000+ points (32,292), and the highest career scoring average (30.1 ppg). He recorded 28 triple‑doubles, was a two‑time Olympic gold medalist (1984, 1992), and his “Flu Game,” 63‑point performance in Game 5 of the 1997 Finals, remains one of the sport’s most iconic moments.
