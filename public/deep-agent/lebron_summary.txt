LeBron James – Quick Reference (≈170 words)

LeBron James is a four‑time NBA champion (Miami Heat 2012, 2013; Cleveland Cavaliers 2016; Los Angeles Lakers 2020) and a four‑time NBA Most Valuable Player (2009, 2010, 2012, 2013). He has been selected to the All‑Star Game 19 times (2005‑2023) and earned 13 All‑NBA First‑Team honors, plus six All‑Defensive Team nods. Internationally, James captured two Olympic gold medals (Beijing 2008, London 2012) and a bronze (Athens 2004).

Notable records include being the youngest player to reach 30,000 career points, holding the all‑time NBA playoff scoring record, and the only player to amass 30,000 points, 10,000 assists, and 9,000 rebounds. He is also the first to win NBA titles with three different franchises and the only player with at least 30 points, eight rebounds, and eight assists in a single season for ten consecutive years (2011‑2020). LeBron’s career averages hover around 27 pts, 7 reb, 7 ast, and he remains a cultural icon and a benchmark for all‑round excellence in basketball.
