LeBron James is a four‑time NBA champion (2012, 2013, 2016, 2020) and a four‑time league MVP (2009, 2010, 2012, 2013). He has been selected to the NBA All‑Star Game 19 times (2005‑2023, missing only 2022 due to injury) and earned a scoring title in 2008, when he led the league with 30.0 points per game. James has also won four NBA Finals MVP awards (matching his championship count) and holds numerous historic milestones: he became the NBA’s all‑time leading scorer, surpassing 38,000 points; he is the first player in league history to record at least 30,000 points, 10,000 rebounds and 10,000 assists; and he ranks among the top five in career triple‑doubles with over 100. His combination of championships, individual honors and record‑breaking achievements cements his status as one of the game’s greatest players.
