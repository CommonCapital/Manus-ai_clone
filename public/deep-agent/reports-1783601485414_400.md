### Summary
- **Definition (ITU‑R Terminology)**  
  - **IoT** = *Internet of Things*: a global infrastructure enabling advanced services by interconnecting physical and virtual “things” using interoperable ICTs.  
  - Emphasises identification, data capture, processing, communication, security and privacy.  

- **Key Publication**  
  - ITU‑T Recommendation **Y.2060** (June 2012) – “Overview of the Internet of Things”.

- **Scope of Y.2060**  
  - Provides IoT terminology, concept, characteristics, high‑level requirements, reference model, and ecosystem/business models.

- **Fundamental Characteristics of the IoT**  
  1. **Interconnectivity** – Anything can be linked to the global ICT infrastructure.  
  2. **Thing‑related services** – Services respect privacy, semantics, and constraints of things.  
  3. **Heterogeneity** – Devices use diverse hardware platforms and networks.  
  4. **Dynamic changes** – Devices can sleep/wake, connect/disconnect, and change context.  
  5. **Enormous scale** – Orders of magnitude more devices than today; shift toward device‑triggered traffic.

- **High‑Level Requirements**  
  - Identification‑based connectivity, interoperability, autonomic networking & services, location‑based capabilities, robust security & privacy, human‑body service quality, plug‑and‑play, and manageability.

- **IoT Reference Model (4 layers + management & security)**  
  1. **Application Layer** – IoT applications.  
  2. **Service Support / Application Support Layer** – Generic (e.g., data processing) and specific support capabilities.  
  3. **Network Layer** – Networking (access, mobility, AAA) and transport capabilities.  
  4. **Device Layer** – Device capabilities (direct/indirect network interaction, ad‑hoc networking, sleep/wake) and gateway capabilities (multi‑interface support, protocol conversion).  
  - **Management capabilities** – FCAPS (fault, configuration, accounting, performance, security) plus generic/specific device management.  
  - **Security capabilities** – Generic & specific functions across application, network, and device layers (auth, authz, confidentiality, integrity, privacy, audit, anti‑virus).

- **Device Types**  
  - **Data‑carrying**, **data‑capturing**, **sensing/actuating**, and **general** devices, each with distinct interaction patterns with the network.

- **IoT Ecosystem & Business Models**  
  - **Roles**: Device Provider, Network Provider, Platform Provider, Application Provider, Application Customer.  
  - **Five business models** (A‑E) illustrate various ownership/combinations of device, network, platform, and application responsibilities, especially from a telecom operator perspective.

- **Related Standards & Series**  
  - Y‑series (global information infrastructure, IP aspects, NGN) – extensive list of sub‑series (Y.100‑Y.899, Y.1000‑Y.1999, etc.).  

- **Intellectual Property Note**  
  - Implementers should consult the ITU‑T IPR database: <http://www.itu.int/ITU-T/ipr/> for any patent claims.
