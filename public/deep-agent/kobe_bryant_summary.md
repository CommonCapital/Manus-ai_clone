Kobe Bryant (1978‑2020) is widely regarded as one of the most accomplished players in NBA history. Over a 20‑year career (1996‑2016) with the Los Angeles Lakers, he captured five NBA championships (2000‑2002, 2009, 2010) and earned two NBA Finals MVP honors (2009, 2010). Although he never won a regular‑season MVP, Bryant was a perennial All‑Star, being selected 18 times (1998‑2016) and earning four All‑Star Game MVP awards. He secured two consecutive scoring titles (2005‑06, 2006‑07) and famously dropped an 81‑point game against the Toronto Raptors in 2006, the second‑highest single‑game total ever. Internationally, Bryant won two Olympic gold medals (2008 Beijing, 2012 London) as a key member of Team USA. His career milestones include retiring as the league’s fourth‑all‑time scorer (33,643 points), becoming the youngest player to reach 30,000 points, and holding the record for most points in a single postseason (284, 2009). Off the court, he earned an Academy Award for his animated short “Dear Basketball” (2018), cementing his legacy as a cultural icon beyond basketball.
