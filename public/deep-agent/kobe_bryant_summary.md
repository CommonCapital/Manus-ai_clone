# Kobe Bryant – Career Accomplishments Summary

**Full Name:** Kobe Bean Bryant (born Kobe Bean Bryant‑Shapiro)
**Born:** August 23, 1978 – Died January 26, 2020
**Position:** Shooting Guard / Small Forward
**NBA Career:** 1996–2016 (Los Angeles Lakers)

## Major Achievements
- **5× NBA Champion** (2000, 2001, 2002, 2009, 2010)
- **2× NBA Finals MVP** (2009, 2010)
- **1× NBA Most Valuable Player** (2008)
- **18× NBA All‑Star** (1998, 2000‑2016)
- **4× NBA All‑Star Game MVP** (2002, 2007, 2009, 2011)
- **11× All‑NBA First/Second Team** (1999‑2002, 2004‑2006, 2009‑2013)
- **9× NBA All‑Defensive Team** (2000‑2001, 2003‑2004, 2006‑2011)
- **2× Olympic Gold Medalist** (2008, 2012) – USA Basketball
- **Career Points:** 33,643 (19.0 ppg) – 4th‑most in NBA history at retirement
- **Scoring Titles:** 2× (2006, 2007)
- **81‑point game** (January 22, 2006) – second‑highest single‑game point total in NBA history
- **60‑point game** in his final NBA appearance (April 13, 2016)

## Notable Milestones & Awards
- First player to win an NBA championship with the same team in his first three seasons (2000‑2002).
- Only player in NBA history with at least 30,000 points, 6,000 rebounds, and 6,000 assists.
- Named to the **NBA 75th Anniversary Team** (2021) and the **NBA 50‑Greatest Players** list (1996).
- Inducted into the **NCAA Hall of Fame** (2009) and the **Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame** (2020, posthumously).
- Won the **Academy Award** for Best Animated Short Film (2020) for “*Dear Basketball*”.

## Post‑Playing Contributions
- Founder of **Mamba Sports Academy** and **Mamba & Mambacita Sports Foundation**.
- Authored multiple books on sports and youth development, including *The Mamba Mentality*.
- Served as an investor and mentor for the NBA’s **Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA)** and youth basketball programs.

## Legacy
Kobe Bryant is widely regarded as one of the greatest players in basketball history, known for his relentless work ethic, “Mamba Mentality” mindset, and impact on the global growth of the sport. His career is marked by championship success, individual accolades, and a lasting influence on players and fans worldwide.
